3D Printed Data Storytelling:
Oregon College Savings Plan

For a lot of the clients, creatives and strategists I've worked with, data is a word that scares them. This exploration aims to solve that by making it tangible and making trends more easily seen.
A project that looks to make data something you can touch, feel, interact with and learn from in a way that makes it more approachable.

  • About Project
  • This project began when we were having internal discussions about how to present data to the client (Oregon College Savings Plan), as normal reporting and visualization techniques went over their head. As we continued to discuss, the logical test case of this technology became working to show OCSP the demographics of their users as well as layering on information about Oregon residents to help further diversification—both with geography and demographics.
  • How to read the map
  • The map below was inspired by a New York Times about when women in different counties have their first child. The color of the county shows the age at which women have their first child (from darkest [Malheur County in the bottom right: 22.7] to lightest [Multnomah County in the top left: 28.8]), while the height of each county shows what percentage of their female population is between 20 and 29 [from the lowest, Wheeler county: 6.7%, to the highest, Benton county at 21.2%]. With this map, we can infer trends about education and the exodus of young women in rural areas, as well as the impact of education on birth age. This allows us for more targeted messaging, leading to diversification in counties other than the Portland metro area.
